Requirements: STR 50 / A (*B), DEX 8 / E

Weapon Type: Greataxe

Attack Type: Slash

Enchantable: Yes

Special: No

*When infused: 80 physical dmg/80 elemental dmg and B scaling with chosen element. Dex scaling changes to C. 

**Gyrm Greataxe** is a **[Greataxe](/Greataxes "Dark Souls 2 Greataxes")** and one of the available **[Weapons](/Weapons "Dark Souls 2 Weapons")** in **[Dark Souls 2](/Dark+Souls+2+Wiki "Dark Souls 2 Wiki")**. Grym Greataxe offers high physical damage and scales with [Strength](/Strength "Dark Souls 2 Strength"). It has a slightly higher weight and dexterity requirement compared to the standard Greataxe.

> A greataxe used by the nomadic Gyrm.   
>   
>  At first glance this greataxe appears crude, but with further examination one sees the fine craftsmanship of Gyrm blacksmiths. Quite heavy for a human, but swung about with reckless abandon by the Gyrm. 

### How to get Gyrm Greataxe in Dark Souls 2

  * Gifted to the player by [Lonesome Gavlan ](/Gavlan "Dark Souls 2 Gavlan") after buying goods for an amount of 16,000 souls.
  * Dropped by [Lonesome Gavlan](/Gavlan "Dark Souls 2 Gavlan") if killed.
  * Random drop from [Gyrm Warrior](/Gyrm+Warrior "Dark Souls 2 Gyrm Warrior") (Axe Version).

### DS2 Gyrm Greataxe Hints and Tips

  * With 120 stamina you can perform four 2-handed R1 attacks, versus only being able to do just 3 with the normal [Greataxe](/Greataxe "Dark Souls 2 Greataxe").
  * Mundane version of this weapon loses all scaling after +1.
  * The rolling 2-handed R1 attack is similar to the Greataxe in Demons Souls.
  * The powerstance moveset is identical to [Lion Greataxe](/Lion+Greataxe "Dark Souls 2 Lion Greataxe"), light attack from Ultra Greatswords and strong attack from Great Hammers.
  * The 1-hand light attack moveset is similar to that of the [Greatsword](/Greatsword "Dark Souls 2 Greatsword"), however it is roughly 10% faster (start to middle of swing in 19 frames vs 21 for the greatsword).
  * 75 Str/12 Dex required to powerstance.

Parameter Bonus: Strength, Dexterity, Magic, Fire, Lightning and Dark bonuses - The scaling multiplier applied to the [Attack: stat]. Scaling quality is from highest to lowest as follows: S/A/B/C/D/E.  
The higher the player's [Str, Dex, Mag, Fire, Lightning, Dark] stat, the higher the [Attack Bonus: Stat] is (found on the player status screen). The higher the scaling letter, the higher the percent multiplier applied to the [Attack: Stat].  
This resulting bonus damage is added to the base physical damage of the weapon and is shown in the equipment screen in blue numbers as a "+ X".  
Attack Type: Defines what kind of moveset the weapon has.  
Physical Damage falls under three categories: [Thrust](/Thrust+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Thrust Damage") (T), [Slash](/Slash+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Slash Damage") (Sl), [Strike](/Strike+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Strike Damage") (St).  
Elemental Damage Types: [Magic](/Magic+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Magic Damage"), [Fire](/Fire+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Fire Damage"), [Lightning](/Lightning+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Lightning Damage"), [Dark](/Dark+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Dark Damage").  
Auxiliary Effects: [Bleed](/Bleed "Dark Souls 2 Bleed"), [Poison](/Poison "Dark Souls 2 Poison"), [Petrification](/Petrification "Dark Souls 2 Petrification"), and [Curse](/Curse "Dark Souls 2 Curse").  
Stability: How well the player keeps stance after being hit.  
Durability: The weapon's HP, when the durability hits 0, the effectiveness of its attacks become weakened to the point of almost uselessness. When an items durability is low, a message will come up saying "Weapon At Risk!".  
Weight: How much the item weighs when equipped.
