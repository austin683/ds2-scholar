# Source: https://darksouls2.wiki.fextralife.com/Blue+Dagger

Requirements: STR 6 / E, DEX 14 / C (*D)

Weapon Type: Dagger

Attack Type: Slash / Thrust

Enchantable: Yes

Special: No

*When infused: 63 physical dmg/38 elemental dmg and B scaling with chosen element. Dex scaling changes to D. 

**Blue Dagger** is a [weapon](/Weapons "Dark Souls 2 Weapons") in **Dark Souls 2**. **Blue Dagger** comes with a unique feature, enhancing spell damage and lowering elemental defenses when equipped. It offers a mix of slashing and thrusting attacks with balanced scaling in strength and dexterity

> Dagger used by fume sorcerers when a battle draws too close for comfort.
> 
> An ordinary-looking dagger laced with magic.
> 
> Strengthens spells, but also increases vulnerability to them.

### How to get Blue Dagger in Dark Souls 2

  * Semi-rare Drop from the [Fume Sorcerer](/Fume+Sorcerer "Dark Souls 2 Fume Sorcerer") enemy in [Brume Tower](/Brume+Tower "Dark Souls 2 Brume Tower").

### DS2 Blue Dagger Hints and Tips

  * Upgraded with [Twinkling Titanite](/Twinkling+Titanite#fex "Dark Souls 2 Twinkling Titanite#fex") to +5.
  * Having the Blue Dagger equipped as your active weapon reduces your own elemental defenses by 200.
  * STR or DEX scaling does not seem to increase with upgrading. However, upgrading does improve the scaling of infusions.
  * Upgrading or infusing does not have any effect on the spell strengthening effect.
  * Spell strengthening effect is approximately 5% and works with all forms of magic.
  * The Blue Dagger's spell strengthening effect stacks with the [Black Witch Domino Mask](/Black+Witch+Domino+Mask "Dark Souls 2 Black Witch Domino Mask")
  * The spell strengthening effect works with all catalysts including weapons that can cast spells such as the [Blue Flame](/Blue+Flame "Dark Souls 2 Blue Flame") and the [Mace of the Insolent](/Mace+of+the+Insolent "Dark Souls 2 Mace of the Insolent")
  * You do not need to meet the stat requirements to benefit from the spell strengthening effect.

Parameter Bonus: Strength, Dexterity, Magic, Fire, Lightning and Dark bonuses - The scaling multiplier applied to the [Attack: stat]. Scaling quality is from highest to lowest as follows: S/A/B/C/D/E.  
The higher the player's [Str, Dex, Mag, Fire, Lightning, Dark] stat, the higher the [Attack Bonus: Stat] is (found on the player status screen). The higher the scaling letter, the higher the percent multiplier applied to the [Attack: Stat].  
This resulting bonus damage is added to the base physical damage of the weapon and is shown in the equipment screen in blue numbers as a "+ X".  
Attack Type: Defines what kind of moveset the weapon has.  
Physical Damage falls under three categories: [Thrust](/Thrust+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Thrust Damage") (T), [Slash](/Slash+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Slash Damage") (Sl), [Strike](/Strike+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Strike Damage") (St).  
Elemental Damage Types: [Magic](/Magic+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Magic Damage"), [Fire](/Fire+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Fire Damage"), [Lightning](/Lightning+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Lightning Damage"), [Dark](/Dark+Damage "Dark Souls 2 Dark Damage").  
Auxiliary Effects: [Bleed](/Bleed "Dark Souls 2 Bleed"), [Poison](/Poison "Dark Souls 2 Poison"), [Petrification](/Petrification "Dark Souls 2 Petrification"), and [Curse](/Curse "Dark Souls 2 Curse").  
Stability: How well the player keeps stance after being hit.  
Durability: The weapon's HP, when the durability hits 0, the effectiveness of its attacks become weakened to the point of almost uselessness. When an items durability is low, a message will come up saying "Weapon At Risk!".  
Weight: How much the item weighs when equipped.
